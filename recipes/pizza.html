New York Italian Pizza Dough

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 ½ cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup bread flour, or more if needed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Directions

  1. Pour the warm water into the pan of a bread machine.
  2. Sprinkle in the salt, and add the flour to the pan so the flour sits on top of the water.
  3. Make a hole in the top of the flour, and spoon the yeast into the hole.
  4. Let the mixture sit for 20 minutes to moisten the flour.
  5. Set the bread machine to the knead setting, and knead the dough for about 10 minutes.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface, and knead in 1/4 cup more flour or as needed, adding the flour about 2 tablespoons at a time, to make a wet, sticky, but elastic dough.
  7. The dough should be "as soft as a baby's bottom." Form the dough into a round shape.
  8. Wipe the inside of a large bowl with olive oil, and place the round ball of dough into the bowl.
  9. Turn the dough over a couple of times so the dough picks up a thin coating of oil.
  10. Cover with a cloth, and refrigerate the dough for 10 hours or overnight.
  11. The next day, allow the dough to come to room temperature before rolling out on a floured surface and adding toppings as desired.
  12. Roll the dough out thin with a thicker edge before adding toppings.
  13. Place a pizza stone into the oven, dust it with a little flour, and preheat the oven to 500 degrees F (260 degrees C).
  14. Bake the pizza on the stone in the preheated oven until the bottom of the crust is browned, 6 to 8 minutes.

Cook's Note

This dough can be made in a bread machine on the quick dough cycle if you prefer. If you do it in a bowl, you really need a dough mixer because this dough will be, and remain, pretty sticky, and it's impossible to work with it with your hands. It's important for the crust texture that it be a very soft, wet dough.
